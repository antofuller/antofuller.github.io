Project Title: Exploring Batch Repetition to Reduce Data IO Costs

Name: Anthony Fuller

E-Mail: anthonyfuller@cmail.carleton.ca

Project Outline: Without terabytes of RAM, the vast amounts of data required to pretrain large deep learning models cannot be stored in memory. With batch repetition, each training data point is sampled multiple times after being loaded from storage. First demonstrated for the supervised training of CNNs, batch repetition has been leveraged for the self-supervised training of ViTs in video processing applications. However, these video applications only use a batch repetition factor (M) of 8. I will experiment with much larger M values and sweep through mask ratios during masked autoencoding (MAE) pretraining.
