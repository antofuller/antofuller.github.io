Project Title: Exploring Batch Repetition to Reduce Data IO Costs

Project Outline:
Batch repetition can significantly decrease pretraining costs. Vast quantities of data are required to pretrain large deep learning models, yet without terabytes of RAM, data cannot be stored in memory; this requires us to load data from storage (i.e., HDDs or SSDs) during pretraining. Typically, a batch of data is loaded from storage, and each sample is used as input for model pretraining before a new batch of data is loaded. With batch repetition, a batch is loaded and used as inputs for model pretraining multiple times before a new batch is loaded. This method was first demonstrated for the supervised training of CNNs and has very recently been leveraged to reduce IO costs for the self-supervised training of ViTs in video processing applications that face high data IO costs.

I will explore the limits of the number of times (M) each sample can be used before the next batch of data is loaded. Hoffer et al. explored M from 2 to 32 and found that data augmentation was crucial in enabling larger M values. However, the two papers extending batch repetition to ViT pretraining only experiment with M up to 8. I will experiment with much larger M values (reducing IO costs) during ViT pretraining and experiment with higher mask ratios (the fraction of patches that are masked from the model in masked autoencoding pretraining)--higher mask ratios increase the amount of effective data augmentation.
